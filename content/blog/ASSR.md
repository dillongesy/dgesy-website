date: "2026-06-26"
excerpt: "A solo developer's perspective on software development in a new-age tech world"
---

It is no mistake to follow standard practices and procedures. Maintaining a high level of security and ensuring critical operations are done in a correct, controlled manner benefits everyone. However, I think as time moves forward in the tech world, there is an untapped creation-focused market. From an outside perspective, in a lot of bigger companies, I feel there's a lot of downtime in a regular work week due to a variety of things. A big one I'd like to focus on is meetings. Some meetings are crucial and very important, such as the concept of progressive elaboration. Ensuring the project is headed in the correct direction is 100% necessary. But in other cases, meetings can exist just for the sake of existing.

As a solo-developer, there is a ludicrous amount of software-work I feel I get done in a non-40 hour work week. No meetings and no waiting for other devs to finish their side of the project. Having the flexibility to work off pure creativity and feed off interest empowers people to learn and create - fast.

Having the flexibility and drive to operate freely from project to project is very convenient. Starting a new project or moving back to an old project is a breath of fresh air. Old projects are good for strengthening past-learned concepts, while starting new projects is another opportunity to learn something different.

This website itself is just one example of this in action. The website serves no purpose. I could learn everything locally and not host it. But who would believe me?

Also worth noting, this is a naive perspective that draws a lot on my personal experience in the IT world while trying to make a software name for myself. These opinions may be changed in the near future, but it's nice to have a record of feeling this way!
